## Skool Post

**Your blog skills write and score content. My SXO skills tell them *what* to write -- and *how* to structure the page.**

Let's be honest: `/blog-write` is amazing. It researches, finds statistics, picks the right template, writes with proper E-E-A-T signals, and delivers a polished article. `/blog-analyze` then scores it on 100 points across 5 categories and catches every issue before you publish.

But here's the blind spot: **both skills start from content.** They don't ask the one question that decides whether your page ranks: What does Google actually reward for this keyword?

**The problem in practice:**

You target "solar panel calculator." Blog Write produces a perfectly researched, beautifully structured 2,500-word guide -- sourced statistics, answer-first formatting, FAQ schema, the works. Blog Analyze gives it 88/100. You publish. Nothing happens.

Why? Because Google's top 3 for that keyword are interactive calculators, not articles. The content was excellent -- but the **page type** was wrong. Blog Write doesn't know that. It writes great articles. That's its job. But it can't tell you that this keyword needs a calculator above the fold and a guide below it.

**That's where SXO comes in.**

`/sxo-analyzer` reads Google's SERPs backwards -- featured snippets, PAA boxes, ads, shopping signals, top-10 page types -- and derives a **User Story**: What does the searcher already know? What emotional state are they in? What's their goal? What's blocking them? Then it compares your page against those signals and shows you every gap in a visual HTML report.

`/sxo-builder` takes that report and turns it into a **before/after wireframe**. It recommends the right page type (Landing Page, Blog, Product Page, or Hybrid), shows your current structure with problems highlighted, and builds the optimized version with ultra-concrete placeholders -- not "add a CTA here" but the exact text, button color, size, and *why* it needs to be there. A designer or developer can implement it without a single follow-up question.

**The 4-skill workflow:**

1. `/sxo-analyzer` -- understand what the market wants before you write a single word
2. `/sxo-builder` -- get a concrete page structure with placeholders for every section
3. `/blog-write` -- create the content pieces that fill those placeholders
4. `/blog-analyze` -- verify quality, E-E-A-T, AI citation readiness before publishing

SXO Analyzer is the **market intelligence layer** -- it tells you what to build. SXO Builder is the **blueprint** -- it shows you how to structure it. Blog Write is the **production engine** -- it creates the content. Blog Analyze is the **quality gate** -- it proves the content is ready.

Without SXO, Blog Write and Blog Analyze are building a great house without checking the zoning laws first. With SXO, you know you're building the right thing before you start.
