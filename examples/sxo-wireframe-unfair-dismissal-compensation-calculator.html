Q1: "What is the average settlement for unfair dismissal?"
A1: “The median award for unfair dismissal at tribunal was £13,541 in 2023/24 (Ministry of Justice statistics). However, most cases settle before tribunal. Settlement amounts typically range from 1–6 months’ gross salary, depending on the strength of your case and your length of service.”

Q2: "How much do you get for unfair dismissal if you win?"
A2: “If you win at tribunal, you receive a basic award (calculated like redundancy pay, max £21,570 in 2025/26) plus a compensatory award for actual financial losses (capped at £118,223 or 52 weeks’ gross pay). Use the calculator above to estimate your basic award.”

Q3: "How often do unfair dismissal claims win?"
A3: “Approximately 5–10% of unfair dismissal claims that reach a full tribunal hearing succeed. However, around 50% of all claims are resolved through settlement (via Acas conciliation or direct negotiation) before reaching a hearing. Most successful claimants never attend a full tribunal.”

Q4: "Is it worth getting a solicitor for unfair dismissal?"
A4: “It depends on the complexity of your case and the amount at stake. For straightforward claims with smaller amounts, Citizens Advice can provide free guidance. For complex cases or larger claims, many employment solicitors offer free initial consultations and no-win-no-fee arrangements.”

Q5: "What is an acceptable settlement offer for unfair dismissal?"
A5: “A reasonable starting point is your calculated basic award plus 3–6 months’ net salary. Use the calculator above to get your basic award figure. Factors that increase acceptable offers: strong evidence, long service, discriminatory dismissal. Factors that decrease: contributory fault, short service.”
